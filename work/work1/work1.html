Problem Overview

Western modern architecture trend has taken great impact to the growth of Chinese cities. Nowadays, Chinese architects realize that we have lost our own culture in this procedure. Today, Chinese architects start to find our own culture origin, which roots in Chinese traditional villages deeply.

Our team wants to learn about what kind of information and data of traditional villages architects need to collect on the internet, and how do they make a Chinese traditional village research before, including what goals they try to achieve when using these tools. We want to determine what opportunities and needs might exist for our platform, so that we can use these findings to help us shape the direction we take in developing our product.

Discovery: Research & Analysis

We conducted two kinds of user reserach Semi-Structured Interview and Survey to explore user mental models and pain points. I recruited 3 participants in the Semi-Structured Interview and 10 participants in the Survey. We collected and summarized the data as affinity diagram.

Key Findings

The significant user needs found within this study are:

100% of architects think the location is one of the main factor when they plan a research journey to decide where to visit.

“Some villages are far away from the city, and lack of public transportation, the distance block my wish to visit their” – Participant 1

More than half participants prefer to visit villages without suffering reconstructions.

“I prefer the village that protected well because it’s vivid”— Participant 3

“I like to visit these villages because I can find childhood memory from it” – Participant 1

All of the architects have great interest on the structure of the architecture

“When I do research, I always take the pictures of interesting architecture structures as a reference for my work. ” – Participant 1

All the architects think photo is the most important media for them to do research

“I usually use photos to record details of the building because of its convenience.” –Participant 1

“If the picture attracts me. I will visit there.” –Participant 2

“I will look through Weibo to see some photos of the villages and choose to visit.” – Participant 3

All the architects would like to select best photos and publish on WeChat Moment after research trip

“I would like to share beautiful pictures with my friends and attract them to come.” –Participant 1

“I want to share amazing architecture to my friends because it is really interesting” -Participant 2

More than half participants like to observe original people in the village

“People is important in the village, it is meaningless if the village is empty.” –Participant 3

“The relationship of people and village is also an important aspect when we research on village” -- Participant2

Design Iteration

Develop: Prototyping

MockUp

After research synthesis and lo-fi prototype procedure, I finished first round mockup and design guide.

Usability Test

After finishing first mockup, I recruit 10 users to test on this clickable prototype with web usability test platform 'Lookup' to get feedback for further iteration. From analysis, I found some opportunities for me to iterate my design:

  • When users enter the home page, they will customly to click the map, though the map is not clickable. It seems that my design is not intuitive and needs to iterate further
  • There is a triangle at the home page which is clickable to zoom the map to the whole page. However, nobody click this button, so it’s discoveriably is really poor, I add a succinct introduction below it in my iteration.
  • The contract of color in the login page doesn’t conform to WCAG AAA criteria. I modify the color in the iteration.

Solution

Iterate the mockup according to the feedback from usability test.

Wrap Up

For my design process, firstly, I defined my product and designed a user research report and recruited 3 architects to ask them questions about the on the report. After collecting the information, I listed their opinions to affinity diagrams to find opportunities in them. And then, I used idea generating methods like Craz-8 to design wireframes of my product and drew it wireframes on Figma. Up next, I drew the clickable prototype of my product and recruited 10 users to do usability test with Lookback. After usability test, I iterate my design and published it.

In this process, I implemented design skills like user research, prototyping iterating and usability test. An interesting insight I encountered is that the map of my first mockup is not clickable. However, I found that in the usability test, a mount of users tried to click the map. So according to usability test, I iterate my design and changed the map to clickable, which is more intuitive to users.

I made a tradeoff when I chose the color. Firstly, I chose some color in a suite, however, I found that when I separate them into foreground color and background color, the color contract did not conform to criteria. Then I change the foreground color to improve it readability.

Due to usability test, I have a confidence that my design can give user relatively good experience on Chinese traditional village research. However, it’s too simple, and needs to add more functions on it.
